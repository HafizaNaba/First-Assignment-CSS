Wild Animals

Wild animals are undomesticated species living in natural habitats, including forests, jungles, and oceans, often studied through wildlife cameras and photography. Key examples include mammals like lions, elephants, and tigers, as well as various bird and reptile species. They are often categorized by habitat or conservation status (e.g., endangered African wild dogWild animals are animals that do not live with humans but instead live in natural places like forests, mountains, deserts, and jungles. They find their own food and survive on their own by hunting or eating plants. Some wild animals, like lions, cheetahs, and wolves, are carnivores, which means they eat meat. Others, like elephants, deer, and zebras, are herbivores and eat plants. There are also omnivores, such as bears, that eat both plants and animals. Wild animals play an important role in maintaining the balance of nature because they are part of the food chain. However, many wild animals are in danger due to deforestation, hunting, and pollution, and some have even become extinct. Therefore, it is very important to protect these animals and their habitats so that they can survive and keep the environment balanced.)

Elephants are the largest living land animals on Earth and are recognized as keystone species due to their significant impact on their environments.
